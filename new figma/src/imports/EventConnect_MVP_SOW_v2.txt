1. Project OverviewEventConnect is a mobile application designed to enhance professional networking at live events (conferences, meetups, industry summits). The application bridges the gap between the physical event experience and digital networking tools by providing attendees with smart matchmaking, real-time proximity networking, structured post-event follow-up, and social calendar features � areas where existing competitors (Brella, Grip, vFairs, Add Eddie, Coatails, Wana, Marshmallow, The Breakfast) have significant gaps.This Statement of Work (v2.0) defines the complete MVP scope based on: updated wireframe sketches (12 screens + 5 unsketched flows), user research interviews (8 respondents, 10 thematic areas), competitive analysis (8+ products), the Kano model prioritization (65 features across 5 categories), and JTBD personas.2. Product Vision & GoalsVision: Make professional event networking intentional, low-anxiety, and high-quality � before, during, and after the event.Core GoalsReduce networking anxiety: Provide visibility into attendee lists, interests, and shared context before the event; offer contextual match tags and mutual validation.Maximize quality connections: Smart matching by job position, interests, and industry � prioritizing relevance over quantity. Swipe mechanics with Like/Hide for user control.Enable real-time networking: Network in Real Time feature with opt-in geolocation, conversation marks, and proximity discovery during event breaks.Solve the post-event follow-up gap: Structured contact saving with hashtag notes, reminder notifications, and 1.5-month post-event archive � the #1 unaddressed gap across all competitors.IRL-first philosophy: The app enhances in-person interaction (not replaces it). QR scanning, location sharing, Free Conversations, and "Find someone to go with" keep the focus on live meetings.B2B delivery model: Organizers import attendees and configure event settings (visibility limits, feature toggles), solving the cold-start problem.3. Technical Constraints & AssumptionsPlatform: Mobile-first (iOS + Android). No web version in MVP.Authentication: Magic link (email-based, one-time, one-device). No passwords.Matching algorithm: Rule-based: Job Position > Interests > Industry. Count of overlaps = ranking. No AI/ML.Real-time messaging: WebSocket or equivalent. Push via FCM/APNs.Geolocation: BLE beacons or WiFi positioning for indoor venue mapping. GPS as fallback.Data retention: 1.5 months post-event active, then archived. Full deletion policy TBD.QR code: Dynamic QR per user per event. Standard format, in-app scanner.Offline: Online-only. Architecture designed for future offline support.Scale target: Events up to 300 attendees. 200-300 is the breakpoint for UX dynamics.Organizer tools: Basic admin: event creation, email import, magic links, feature toggles, visibility limits.Integrations: Google Calendar export.
